Six documented data and method limitations
1
Sample size
500K rows = 0.116% of the 430M production dataset. Patterns may not fully generalise. Production deployment requires revalidation at scale.
2
NLP method — keyword matching
Domain tagging uses regex, not embeddings. Complex multi-domain phrases may be mis-tagged. Cross-domain language is underrepresented.
3
Sentiment method — VADER
General-purpose analyser, not trained on clinical text. Health phrases like "you are at risk" may score unexpectedly. A clinically fine-tuned model would improve Phase 11.
4
Risk label derivation
Label derived from status_assessment text parsing. Accuracy depends on template consistency. Not validated against external clinical ground truth.
5
Cross-sectional design
Each row is a snapshot — no longitudinal tracking. Cannot track individual patient trajectories. Trend analysis requires patient ID and timestamp data.
6
Fixed features
dif_nutri is fixed (1 unique value) and was removed. Nutrition domain provides zero ML discriminative information. This is a data collection issue, not a modelling choice.
